01
Power-Off Approach Technique
Simulating engine failure from safe height · distance management · attitude & speed control
Theory What Is a Power-Off Approach?

A power-off approach simulates engine failure in the descent by reducing power to idle from a position where a safe landing is assured without further power application. The descent continues on a glide to landing, with the nose attitude maintained to preserve airspeed.

Core Rule — Power-Off
Pitch attitude → airspeed
Power → vertical speed
Extend configuration (flaps) → lower nose to maintain speed

Never stretch the glide by raising the nose. In power-off descent, raising the nose causes loss of airspeed and imminent stall. Use S-turns or extend flaps to manage distance.

Procedure Glide Speed Adjustment for Headwind

In a headwind, increase glide speed slightly to maximise glide distance over the ground:

Glide Speed = Standard Best Glide + ½ Headwind Component (up to maximum of VY)

For PA-28 standard best glide is approximately 73 KIAS. If headwind is 20 kts, add 10 kts: target 83 KIAS (but do not exceed VY 79 KIAS). In this case, increase to 79 KIAS.

⚠
No Tailwind Power-Off Approaches
Do NOT attempt power-off approaches with a tailwind. The ground distance shrinks dangerously. Always plan power-off approaches on the headwind runway.
Technique Configuration & Trim During Descent

As configuration is extended (flaps selected), simultaneously LOWER THE NOSE to maintain speed:

  • After each flap selection, adjust pitch attitude down to maintain target speed on the ASI
  • Retrim immediately after the pitch change — do not carry excessive stick force
  • Flap extension increases drag — the aircraft will try to descend faster. Lower nose attitude prevents speed loss
  • On final, perform required final check: "Final check: Flaps full set"

Go-Around from Power-Off Approach
Standard recovery procedure · flap management · carb heat
Procedure Standard Go-Around Callout

If a go-around is required from a power-off approach, execute the standard G/A procedure:

1
Going around
2
Full power
3
Flaps ≤1 or less
4
G/A attitude
5
Positive ROC
SOP Before advancing throttle for full power on go-around: carb heat COLD (OFF). After positive rate at 400 ft AGL: "Max continuous power" §1.26
Warning Critical SOP Items
  • Final Check on Power-Off Approach: "Final check: Flaps full set" — this must still be performed even without engine power. Do not skip it.
  • Carb Heat Before Go-Around Power: Ensure carb heat is COLD before applying full throttle. Hot intake reduces engine power.
  • Touch-and-Go After Power-Off Approach: After landing, retract flaps to take-off position (0° or partial) and RETRIM before applying power for take-off roll.
Summary Common Error Patterns
  • Forgetting to adjust attitude and trim after extending configuration
  • Trying to adjust final flightpath with attitude instead of power/config change
  • Not using peripheral vision during flare when setting up power-off touch-down
  • Positioning controls for crosswind instead of maintaining steady bank toward wind
  • Underestimating the importance of headwind glide speed adjustment
