Solo Check Framework
No new items · instructor says very little · evaluation mode · go-around at own initiative is key criterion
Assessment What the Solo Check Is NOT

The solo check is NOT a lesson. No new items are introduced. The instructor is not teaching. They are evaluating.

  • No new procedures — all techniques learned in DGH 01–DGH 09
  • Instructor says very little — talking uses precious mental capacity. Expect silence.
  • Instructor is observing, not supporting. Do not wait for advice.
  • Flying is your responsibility. Make decisions. Apply corrections confidently.
  • If you make an error, expect the instructor to NOT correct it. This tests your own awareness.
Mindset Managing Anxiety & Staying in Command

Anxiety about solo check is normal. But it does NOT help your performance. In fact, it reduces it.

Core Principle
Focus on doing the job. Not on the pressure. You already know how to do this.
  • You have done this before. DGH 09 was nearly identical — just with more instructor input.
  • Trust your preparation. You have flown multiple circuits. The aircraft will behave the same way.
  • Apply pitch and power corrections confidently. Be in command.
  • Use all crosscheck techniques. ASI, altimeter, heading, bank angle, vertical speed.
  • Scan for traffic constantly. Situational awareness is non-negotiable for solo.
Critical GO-AROUND AT OWN INITIATIVE — The Key Solo Criterion

If anything does not look right on approach, go-around.

⚠
Decision Point
NOT implementing a go-around at your own initiative when needed is an ERROR THAT WILL PRECLUDE SOLO. This is the most important criterion of the entire check.

Red flags for go-around:

  • Approach not stabilised (speed, altitude, or descent rate wavering)
  • Positioned too high on approach (cannot make landing within tolerances)
  • Excessive drift or bank during final (wind or technique issue)
  • Traffic or other aircraft on runway
  • Ground environment looks unsafe (debris, animals, crosswind beyond limits)
  • Any doubt or hesitation — when in doubt, go around
Remember
Going around is a SAFE choice. Landing an unstable approach is a risky choice. The check assesses whether you make the safe choice.
Procedure After Satisfactory Check — Release to First Solo

If the check assessment is satisfactory (all criteria met, including proper go-around decision-making), the instructor will exit the aircraft and release you for first solo flight(s).

  • The instructor will brief your solo flights (circuit pattern, radio frequency, emergency procedures)
  • You will conduct multiple circuits solo
  • Solo operations follow standard EKRK circuit procedures
  • This is the beginning of your independent flying career
