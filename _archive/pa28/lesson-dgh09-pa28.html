Consolidation Framework
All previously learned skills · flapless approach · mental attitude toward autonomy
Theory Consolidation Is Not New Learning

By DGH 09, all circuit procedures must be thoroughly consolidated. No new items are introduced except the flapless approach. The focus shifts from learning to demonstrating reliable execution. The instructor will be less involved, giving you more responsibility for decisions.

Mental Shift
You already know what to do. Now you must do it reliably and independently.
Technique Flapless Approach (Flap Failure Procedure)

If flaps are inoperative (mechanical failure), the flapless approach is performed:

  • Minimum approach speed: 1.3 × VS1 clean = 1.3 × 50 = 65 KIAS
  • Critical check: Ensure runway available is sufficient. Flapless landing distance is significantly longer than normal.
  • Attitude: Flapless approach attitude is slightly more nose-up than normal approach
  • Touch-down speed: Higher than normal (65 KIAS vs. typical 50 KIAS)
  • Landing roll: Expect longer landing distance. Do not attempt flapless on short runways.
Mindset Increasing Autonomy — Instructor Role Changes

Mental Attitude: Be aggressive and stay in command. The instructor will NOT support unless genuinely needed. Do not expect the instructor to catch your errors — this is your flight now.

  • Making own decisions and implementing them is the goal
  • Go-around at own initiative when approach is unstable or does not look right
  • Pitch and power corrections applied confidently without prompting
  • Circuit management: Your responsibility to manage altitude, heading, speed
  • The instructor is evaluating, not teaching. Talking uses mental capacity.

SOP Callouts & Critical Procedures
Standard callouts · flap management · touch-and-go coordination
Procedure Standard Circuit Callouts (CAPA SOP §1.26)

Callouts are mandatory and must be practiced:

  • "My controls - my trim" / "Your controls - your trim" — at handover
  • "Speed [XX] kts. Checked - Flaps [X]" → "Flaps [X] Set" — after flap selection
  • "Final clear" — on base, checking traffic on final
  • "Final Clear - Lining up RWY [XX]" → "Heading Indicator Checked - RWY [XX] Identified" — on final approach
  • "Final check: Flaps full set" — before landing (PA28 has no propeller fine)
  • "Going around - Full power - Flaps 1 or less - G/A attitude - Positive ROC - Safe speed - Flaps up" — standard go-around
Critical Flap Management — Touch-and-Go Landing

After landing in a touch-and-go:

  • Retract flaps to take-off position (0° or partial) — flaps up for climb-out
  • RETRIM immediately — the aircraft will pitch down as flaps are retracted
  • BEFORE advancing power for take-off roll, ensure trim is set for take-off configuration
  • Many student errors occur because trim is not reset before applying power
⚠
Trim Before Power
The aircraft will want to pitch down when you retract flaps. If you do not retrim, applying power will cause a sudden pitch-down as the trim takes effect during the take-off roll.
Summary Reinforcement of Previously Learned Procedures

DGH 09 consolidates all techniques from DGH 01 through DGH 08:

  • Standard circuit procedures & callouts
  • Approach techniques (power-on & power-off)
  • Go-around drills & execution at own initiative
  • Flare & landing technique
  • Touch-and-go flap management & trim coordination
  • Situational awareness & traffic scanning

All common errors from previous lessons must be corrected. This is the final check before solo assessment.
