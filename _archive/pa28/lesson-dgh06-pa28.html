Circuit Standardisation
Visual marks · repeatable legs · approach setup
SOP
Standard Circuit Legs with Configuration Callouts
KEY PRINCIPLE
The landing circuit is the "receipt for a good landing." A stabilised final of proper length gives maximum assistance for a safe landing. Make circuits as standardised as possible using pre-selected visual marks rather than DG hunting.
DOWNWIND
  • Abeam threshold: reduce power to descent power
  • Speed 90 kts. Checked - Flaps 1 (Flaps 1 selected)
  • Final clear — check for traffic on final approach
BASE
  • Turn base at appropriate point (pre-selected visual landmark)
  • Speed 80 kts. Checked - Flaps 2 (Flaps 2 selected, 25°)
  • Maintain descent and approach speed
FINAL
  • Final clear from base — confirm runway is clear
  • When landing is assured: Final check: Flaps full set
  • Maintain 75 KIAS on final approach
  • Nose attitude controls airspeed; power controls glidepath (descent rate)
2
Approach Control & Flare Technique
Power vs. attitude · peripheral vision · ground closure rate
TECHNIQUE
Approach Configuration & Control

Normal Approach Config: Flaps 25°, 75 KIAS. Nose attitude provides the correct flight path for approach; power controls glidepath (descent rate).

  • Maintain 75 KIAS on final using pitch attitude
  • Adjust descent rate using power — add/reduce power smoothly
  • Do NOT adjust glidepath with attitude — use power only
  • Trim to remove control column pressure
⚠
COMMON ERROR
Trying to adjust final glidepath with attitude instead of power leads to unstable approaches and altitude/speed conflicts.
TECHNIQUE
Flare — Maintaining Height & Descent Rate Control

At the runway threshold, gradually raise the nose to reduce descent rate. Maintaining same height — progressively raise nose as aircraft decelerates, reducing ground closure rate from rapid descent to near zero by touchdown.

  • Use peripheral vision to judge height and descent rate — not forward view down runway
  • Peripheral vision detects rate of ground closure more sensitively
  • Judging rate of descent in the flare — the flare gradually arrests descent rate
  • Progressive nose-up pitch as airspeed decreases reduces descent rate smoothly
  • Target touchdown in middle third of runway with minimal descent rate

Go-Around Drills & Crosswind Landings
Decision-making · carburettor heat · crosswind technique
SOP
Go-Around Decision & Execution

Student Responsibility: The student is responsible for deciding to go-around if things don't look right. Once started, the go-around must NOT be abandoned.

  • Conduct go-around if: unstable approach, excessive descent rate, not aligned with runway, traffic on final, or gusty winds
  • Execute standard go-around callout: Going around - Full power - Flaps 1 or less - G/A attitude - Positive ROC - Safe speed - Flaps up
  • CRITICAL: Carb heat must be COLD (OFF) before applying full power
  • At 400 ft AGL: Max continuous power
TECHNIQUE
Crosswind Landing Technique

Maintain a small, steady bank toward the wind (into-wind wing slightly low). Do NOT position flight controls for crosswind — fly the aircraft using attitude and bank angle!

  • Establish small bank (<5°) into wind during approach
  • Maintain coordinated flight with rudder
  • Reduce bank as you slow down on final
  • Apply slight into-wind aileron during flare to keep wings level relative to runway
  • Fly nose aligned with runway; let wings align naturally with bank

Common Errors
Attitude Flying · Landing Technique · Decision-Making
ERRORS
Critical Mistakes to Avoid
  • Forgetting attitude flying in circuit: Using power to control speed or attitude to control descent rate leads to oscillation and unstable approaches.
  • Forgetting to adjust attitude & trim after configuration change: After extending flaps, nose attitude changes. Must re-trim and re-establish pitch for desired speed.
  • Trying to adjust final glidepath with attitude: Power controls descent rate; attitude controls speed. Using attitude for glidepath adjustment causes unstable approaches.
  • Not using peripheral vision during flare: Forward vision down the runway is poor for height judgment. Peripheral vision detects ground closure rate better.
  • Forgetting final check: PA28 final check when landing is assured: Final check: Flaps full set. This confirms landing configuration.
  • Positioning flight controls for crosswind: Fly the aircraft with attitude and bank; do NOT pre-position aileron/rudder for wind correction. Let coordinated flight naturally align the aircraft.
  • Late or no decision to go-around: Go-around decision must be made early and decisively. If things don't look right, commit to go-around.
  • Forgetting carburettor heat COLD during go-around: Carb heat must be OFF before applying full power. Critical engine damage risk if forgotten.
