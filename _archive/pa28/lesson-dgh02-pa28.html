1
Mindset for This Lesson
This is the first lesson with real demands — do the procedure
Briefing
Mental Approach to DGH 02

This lesson introduces real flying demands. You will be asked to perform multiple manoeuvres and follow procedures. The critical mindset is: Do the procedure. Do not think about whether it is right or wrong. The instructor is there to ensure nothing goes wrong.

Procedure Technique
"Sing the song" — call out procedures loudly, make control and switch inputs, and DO the procedure. Confidence is essential. Hesitation will degrade performance.

Anxiety about performing correctly is normal. Channel that anxiety into energy for precise procedure execution. Callouts are your anchor — they keep you on track and let the instructor monitor your state of mind.

2
Speed Changes — Flap Verification
ALWAYS verify speed before selecting flaps down or up
SOP
Speed Change Callout Procedure

This callout is mandatory for every speed change:

CAPA SOP
Speed [XX] kts. Checked - Flaps [X]

Then confirm:

CAPA SOP
Flaps [X] Set

Why this procedure: You verify on the airspeed indicator that you are at the correct speed BEFORE deploying flaps. Deploying flaps at too high a speed can damage the aircraft. VFE (flap extended speed) for PA28 is 103 kt. Always check airspeed is ≤ VFE before lowering flaps.

3
Anticipation in Flight
The aircraft does not respond instantly — think ahead
Technique
Control Input Timing

Anticipate aircraft reaction to inputs. When you change power or attitude, the aircraft does not respond instantly. A lag exists between your input and the aircraft's response.

  • Climbs: Apply power and pitch attitude for climb. Altitude begins rising after a slight delay. Anticipate level-off before reaching target altitude — begin the leveling manoeuvre about 100 ft below target
  • Descents: Apply descent attitude and reduce power. Descent rate builds over time. To stop descent, begin raising nose before reaching target altitude
  • Speed changes: Change attitude, monitor airspeed, allow aircraft to accelerate/decelerate, then select flaps

Anticipation comes from continuous crosschecking. If you are climbing at +500 ft/min from 1500 ft to 2000 ft, glance at altimeter around 1950 ft and begin level-off. This prevents overshooting.

4
Turns — Think Bank Angle
Turns are preferably done with DG/AI covered, forcing attitude/visual marks
Technique
Turn Execution

Think in bank angle terms, not aileron input terms. A 15° bank = shallow turn. A 20° bank = standard rate turn. A 25° bank = steep turn.

  • Set a bank angle by aiming the wing against a natural visual mark (cloud, terrain feature)
  • Maintain that bank angle with crosscheck (95% nose attitude, quick glance at horizon to check roll)
  • Do not change bank angle during the turn unless correcting — hold it steady
  • Roll out when heading approaches target by reducing bank angle

Flying the turn without DG/AI: If the instructor covers these instruments, you must fly the turn using visual reference and attitude. This is excellent training for basic stick-and-rudder flying. Trust your natural horizon reference.

5
Go-Around Procedure
Multiple items in the CORRECT ORDER — drill this on ground BEFORE flight
Critical Procedure
Go-Around Callout & Execution

Go-around procedure must be performed in EXACT ORDER:

CAPA SOP
Going around - Full power - Flaps 1 or less - G/A attitude - Positive ROC - Safe speed - Flaps up

Then at 400 ft AGL:

CAPA SOP
Max continuous power

IMPORTANT for PA28: When advancing to full power on go-around, ensure carburettor heat is COLD before applying full power (fuel mixture cooling effect prevents carburettor icing).

Ground Briefing Required
Drill this callout on the ground BEFORE flight. Say it aloud 5 times. Understand the meaning of each item. The go-around must be automatic — you will not have time to think in the actual situation.
6
Emergency Procedures Introduction
Carburettor icing and engine failure on take-off
Emergency
Carburettor Icing & Engine Failure
⚠
Carburettor Icing
Recognition: Engine roughness, power loss, or RPM reduction. Procedure: Apply carburettor heat (pull carb heat knob) in glide or reduced power. If symptoms persist, full heat application. Never apply full carb heat at full power.
⚠
Engine Failure on Take-Off
Before VR (rotate speed ≈60 kt): Close throttle, apply brakes. STOP before reaching end of runway. After VR: Maintain control, fly best glide, land ahead. Never attempt to turn back to runway — you will lose altitude and crash.
7
Control Feel & Light Hands
2–3 fingers only — do not grip with white knuckles
Technique
How to Hold the Controls

Hold the control column with 2–3 fingers only when feeling aircraft responses. A white-knuckle grip masks control feel and prevents you from sensing how the aircraft responds to inputs.

  • Use thumb and 2 fingers with light but deliberate touch
  • Feel the control pressures and aircraft feedback
  • Sense when the aircraft is approaching a stall (back pressure increases)
  • Feel speed changes through elevator trim

A tight grip numbs all feedback. In later training, you will perform stalls and recoveries. You cannot feel a stall developing if you grip with a fist. Build good habits now.
