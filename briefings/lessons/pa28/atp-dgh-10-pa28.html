Instructor Self-Briefing
Evaluation protocol · go-around non-negotiable · solo release criteria
1
Evaluation Only
No instruction, no corrections, no advice unless dangerous
EvaluationSolo Release Assessment

This is an evaluation lesson ONLY. No instruction, no corrections, no advice unless dangerous. Student must demonstrate everything from DGH 00–09 independently. Reassure student: anxiety is normal and does not indicate unreadiness.

SOLO RELEASE IS WITHHELD IF: Student does not initiate go-around at own initiative when approach is unsatisfactory. This is non-negotiable.

SOPGo-around self-initiation is a release prerequisite§1.18
Critical Gate
If student hesitates or fails to initiate go-around when approach is poor, do NOT release.

The Briefing
Ground preparation for evaluation flight
1
Briefing
Evaluation lesson — no new demands
BriefingEvaluation Lesson Brief

Brief: this is an evaluation lesson only. No new demands. You will fly to the same standard as DGH 09. I will observe; no guidance. Focus: consistent circuits, independent go-around decisions.

Brief the solo plan: After successful evaluation, your first solo is next. What to expect: physically lighter aircraft (no instructor weight), same procedures, same calls, same circuits. Solo is just the circuit practice you've been doing — but alone.

SOPGo-around: must be YOUR decision, not mine§1.18
2
Key Concepts
What makes a successful solo release
ConceptIndependent Go-Around Decision
Non-Negotiable Criterion
You must initiate go-around at your own decision when the approach is unsatisfactory. I will not tell you. This decision must be yours.
  • Anxiety on evaluation: completely normal
  • Performance may be slightly lower — that is okay
  • Same circuit standard as DGH 09 applies
  • Go-around decision: yours alone
  • All checks and calls: same as every lesson
ChecklistBriefing Coverage Checklist
