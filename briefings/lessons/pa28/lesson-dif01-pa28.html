T-Panel Scan Architecture
Understanding instrument arrangement and primary vs. supporting instruments
THEORY Basic T Layout & Philosophy
The PA-28 T-panel is arranged so that the attitude indicator (AI) is centrally positioned, with supporting performance instruments around it: ASI (left), Altimeter (right), and Directional Indicator (DI) below. This arrangement keeps primary attitude information at the pilot's focal point.
Core Principle
The AI during instrument flight is exactly like the natural horizon during VFR. Treat it identically. 90% of your attention stays on the AI, with brief cross-checks (½ second max) of ONE other instrument, then back to AI.
TECHNIQUE Detect → Correct → Verify Cycle
  • Detect on performance instrument: Altimeter shows climb. You see this in a ½-second glance.
  • Correct on AI: Lower the wing bar on the AI slightly. Make ONE small pitch correction.
  • Verify on performance: Glance back at altimeter to see if descent has started. Return to AI immediately.
    • ⚠
    Crosscheck Discipline
    Never scan 2+ other instruments before returning to AI. This breaks instrument scan discipline and causes control loss. Even on the ground, the FI watches your eye movements.
    2
    180° Turn to Clear IMC
    Precision turns under the hood using basic T scan
    TECHNIQUE Rate 1 Turn Setup & Execution
  • Bank angle for Rate 1: Approximately 15° bank. At typical training speeds, this gives 3°/sec turn rate = 60 seconds for 180°.
  • Establish on AI: Roll the aircraft to 15° bank on the AI and hold steady.
  • Monitor heading: Glance DI for heading progress. Do NOT stare at DI to maintain heading — maintain wings level on AI.
  • Maintain altitude: Altimeter must stay within ±100 ft. Pitch slightly to maintain level attitude. Power holds altitude during turn.
  • Roll out: When DI approaches desired heading, roll out to level on AI. Cross-check with altimeter and ASI.
    • 3
    VDF Navigation: QDM & QDR
    Understanding ATC-provided magnetic headings and bearings for return to EKRK
    TECHNIQUE QDM (To) vs. QDR (From)
    QDM: Magnetic heading TO the station. ATC reads your transmission and provides the heading you must fly to reach the station (e.g., "QDM 350, expect the station in 12 minutes").
    QDR: Magnetic bearing FROM the station. This tells you where you are relative to the station radial (e.g., "You are QDR 170 from EKRK" = you are on the 170° radial, so fly 350° to return).
    Practical Use
    During return to EKRK at lesson end, ATC will provide QDM guidance. Fly the heading on the DI. Maintain steady altitude and airspeed on AI + altimeter + ASI. Expect radio navigation guidance throughout the approach.

    CAPA SOP Callouts
    Standard phraseology for control handover and major manoeuvres
    SOP Control Handover & Trim
    CALLOUT My controls - my trim when you take the aircraft. Your controls - your trim when the student takes control. SOP § 1.26
    These callouts occur at the start and during transitions. They confirm who is flying and remind you to reset trim immediately when taking control.
    SOP Stall Warning — Hard Warning Callout
    ⚠
    STALL WARNING = HARD WARNING
    Under foggles, any stall warning must be treated as a hard warning (per CAPA SOP § 1.25). Immediate go-around: Going around - Full power - Flaps 1 or less - G/A attitude - Positive ROC - Safe speed - Flaps up then Max continuous power at 400 ft AGL.
    5
    Flight Sequence & Debrief
    Lesson flow and key learning points
    TECHNIQUE In-Flight Sequence
    Phase 1: Scan Establishment
  • Climb to 1500 ft AGL under visual reference
  • Don foggles — transition to T-panel instruments only
  • Establish level flight — practice AI scan: 90% on AI, glance altimeter, return
  • Small turns (10° bank) to confirm crosscheck technique
    • Phase 2: Control Manoeuvres
  • Climbs at selected power — maintain heading ±20° on DI using AI roll
  • Descents at selected ROD — hold altitude tolerance ±200 ft
  • Speed changes — pitch attitude governs speed, power governs altitude
    • Phase 3: 180° Turns
  • Execute 180° turn to starboard at Rate 1 (15° bank)
  • Maintain altitude ±200 ft, heading accuracy ±20° at roll-out
  • Repeat to port
    • Phase 4: Return on VDF
  • Defoggles
  • Request QDM guidance to EKRK
  • Fly heading on DI under basic instrument reference
  • Descend to circuit altitude maintaining scan discipline
  • Standard approach and landing
    • DEBRIEF Key Debrief Items
