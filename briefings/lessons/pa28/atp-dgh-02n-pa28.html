Flight
How to conduct this lesson in the air
Flight Night Circuit Consolidation

DGH 02N is pure circuit consolidation. The student operates independently — instructor evaluates and intervenes for safety only. Focus: consistent stable approaches at correct PAPI glidepath (minimum 2 white/2 red), smooth flare with correct height judgement using peripheral vision, and independent go-around decisions.

If the student consistently accepts 4×red approaches, more dual is needed before night solo release. The student must demonstrate the go-around decision independently — this is non-negotiable for solo release.

2
Common Errors
Anticipate and address these during DGH 02N
Errors Common Mistakes — DGH 02N
✕ Forgetting BL 5-19 night equipment requirements
✕ Losing orientation during night taxi
✕ Lowering nose post-takeoff as if daytime (should use instruments for horizon immediately after lift-off)
✕ Excessive instrument fixation — should blend visual and instrument
✕ Losing depth/distance judgment at night
✕ Losing sense of time passage
✕ Optical illusions on final — black hole effect
✕ Flaring too low (black hole illusion)
✕ Correcting glidepath with pitch instead of power

1
Briefing
What to cover during the ground brief
Briefing Pre-Flight Ground Preparation

This is a consolidation lesson. Brief specific weaknesses identified in DGH 01N. Key items to re-cover: PAPI discipline (2W/2R = on glidepath; 4R = go around immediately), flare height (peripheral vision, "maintain same height"), and go-around decision criteria (unstabilised = go around, no exception).

2
Key Concepts
Core ideas to reinforce during this lesson
Concept PAPI & Flare Discipline
PAPI Discipline
2 white / 2 red = on glidepath. 4 red = go around immediately. Never accept 4×red. This is non-negotiable.
  • Flare height: peripheral vision — look to runway end, sense runway edges in peripheral vision
  • Mantra: "maintain same height" as aircraft decelerates
  • Go-around decision: unstabilised = go around, no exception
  • Circuit procedure: all legs, calls, checks followed throughout
Checklist Briefing Coverage Checklist
  • Review DGH 01N debrief weak points
  • PAPI discipline — 2W/2R glidepath; never 4R
  • Flare technique — peripheral vision, "same height" mantra
  • Independent go-around decision criteria
  • Night circuit procedure — all legs, calls, checks

Post-Flight Review
Questions to guide the de-brief discussion
Debrief De-Brief Discussion Points
1 PAPI discipline: Were approaches consistently at correct PAPI glidepath?
2 Go-around decisions: Were all go-around decisions made independently?
3 Flare height: Was flare height consistent? Correct visual cues used?
4 Situational awareness: Was situational awareness maintained throughout?
5 Self-assessment: Can the student identify their own weak points?

Criteria to progress to Night Solo
Progress Ready for Night Solo Circuits
✓ Stable approaches (2W/2R minimum) on ALL circuits
✓ Independent go-around decisions demonstrated
✓ No 4×red approaches accepted
✓ Flare height correctly judged
✓ Circuit procedure followed throughout
