01
Precision & Non-Precision Approaches
ILS approach · LCTR approach · MDA discipline · decision height compliance
Manoeuvre ILS Approach — Precision Approach

Intercept localiser from below (for glide slope capture). Monitor both LOC and GS needles. Cross-check altimeter at all fixes. DH compliance is mandatory.

  • Establish approach configuration per ATC clearance
  • Intercept localiser from below — glide slope will capture automatically
  • Maintain centred LOC and GS needles — pitch and power control
  • Cross-check altimeter against approach chart at each fix
  • Maintain approach speed and descent rate for stable descent
  • At DH, must have required visual reference or execute missed approach immediately
  • Do NOT descend below DH without required visual reference
Manoeuvre LCTR Approach — Non-Precision Approach

Track the localiser inbound. Descend in steps to MDA. Level off at MDA and maintain until visual contact. Do NOT descend below MDA without required visual reference.

  • Intercept localiser — track inbound on final approach course
  • Descend in steps to MDA as per approach plate
  • Level off at MDA — maintain altitude precisely
  • Maintain look-out for visual contact with runway or approach lights
  • Maintain MDA until landing runway clearly in sight
  • Do NOT descend below MDA unless landing is assured
  • If visual contact not obtained by MAP, execute missed approach

02
Circling & Missed Approach
Visual circling at MDA · break-off rules · missed approach execution
Manoeuvre Circling Approach Procedure

After breaking off from instrument approach, circle visually to land on different runway. Maintain visual contact with airfield. Do NOT break off circling before visual contact obtained.

  • Complete non-precision approach to MDA
  • Obtain visual contact with airport/landing area before breaking off
  • Maintain visual contact with airfield throughout circling manoeuvre
  • Maintain MDA during circling — do NOT descend until on final to landing runway
  • Do NOT descend below MDA until established on landing runway final approach
  • During base/final turn to landing runway, can descend below MDA when landing is assured
  • Avoid steep turns and aggressive manoeuvring — smooth, controlled circling
!
Break-Off Rule — Critical
Never break off circling before obtaining visual contact with the airfield. This is a hard rule — if you cannot see the runway environment at MDA, execute missed approach immediately.
Manoeuvre Missed Approach Procedure

Fly the published missed approach procedure exactly. Must be memorised before flight. "Going around - Full power - Flaps 1 or less - G/A attitude - Positive ROC - Safe speed - Flaps up" + "Max continuous power" then follow published track and altitude.

  • At decision point (DH or MAP), if visual contact not obtained: execute missed approach
  • Reduce flaps to 1 or less — reduce drag immediately
  • Apply full power — establish positive rate of climb
  • Establish go-around attitude — appropriate pitch and bank angle
  • Climb to safe speed before raising flaps completely
  • Follow published missed approach track and altitude precisely
  • Communicate with ATC — inform of missed approach status
  • Do NOT improvise — follow the published procedure exactly
!
Climbing During Break-Off
Watch the VSI carefully during break-off. Many pilots unconsciously climb when initiating go-around. Ensure you are in go-around configuration BEFORE climbing.
SOP Pre-Flight Preparation
  • Fly this lesson on the available CAPA student simulator before actual lesson
  • Memorise missed approach procedure before flight — do NOT rely on looking at plate during manoeuvre
  • Verify approach plate frequencies, altitudes and course headings
  • Brief all approach transitions with instructor — know expectations clearly
  • Understand ATC communication intentions and standard phraseology for each approach
