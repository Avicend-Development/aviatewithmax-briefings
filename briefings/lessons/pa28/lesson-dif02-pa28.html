Rated Descent Control
Establishing a target rate of descent and maintaining it with precision
THEORY Rated Descent Principle
A rated descent is intentional — you decide what rate of descent you want (e.g., 500 fpm) and hold it. Many students default to whatever descent rate the aircraft falls into naturally. That is NOT a rated descent.
Key Principle
Power controls rate of descent. Nose attitude (pitch) controls airspeed. Set pitch for desired speed, set power for desired ROD, then wait for the aircraft to stabilise before adjusting further.
TECHNIQUE Power Corrections & Patience
  • Select target ROD: "I want to descend at 500 fpm."
  • Set power to achieve that rate. Make small adjustments (50 rpm at a time).
  • Do NOT chase the VSI. VSI lags behind actual change by 6–8 seconds.
  • Set attitude, set power, WAIT, observe result, fine-tune only if needed.
  • Over-correcting on power causes overshoot (descending too fast, then climbing too fast).
    • ⚠
    Patience is Discipline
    The temptation to make constant small corrections is strong. Resist. One power adjustment, then observe. If still off target after 10 seconds, adjust again. Not every VSI needle twitch requires action.

    VOR Exercises on Instruments
    Tracking and intercept using CDI crosscheck whilst maintaining AI primary scan
    TECHNIQUE VOR Intercept & Tracking
  • TUNE frequency to ACTIVE on NAV 1.
  • SELECT appropriate OBS course.
  • IDENT aurally (morse code).
  • Fly intercept heading. When CDI needle begins to move smoothly, roll out with wind correction angle on the radial.
  • Maintain course on AI. Glance CDI briefly (½ sec) to verify. Return to AI.
  • Detect deviation on CDI → Correct on AI roll → Verify on CDI.
    • Instructor Note
    Ensure student maintains 90% attention on AI even whilst tracking VOR. CDI is a supporting instrument. Never look at CDI to maintain heading — maintain wings level on AI.

    180° Level Turn: Progress Check
    Demonstrating improved precision with ±100 ft / ±20° tolerance
    TECHNIQUE Execution for Progress Check
    Establish 15° bank (Rate 1 turn). Monitor altitude on altimeter — must stay within ±100 ft during turn. Roll out when DI shows desired heading ±20°. This is a progress check: can you complete a turn with better control?
    Improvement Target
    DIF 01 tolerance was ±200 ft / ±20° hdg. DIF 02 raises the bar to ±100 ft / ±20° hdg. Demonstrates tighter control on altitude during manoeuvres.

    Stall Exercises to First Warning Only
    Hard warning policy under instruments — immediate go-around
    WARNING Stall Warning = Hard Warning (SOP § 1.25)
    ⚠
    HARD WARNING POLICY
    Under foggles, any stall warning must be reacted upon immediately. Do NOT continue toward actual stall. On hearing/feeling stall warning: Going around - Full power - Flaps 1 or less - G/A attitude - Positive ROC - Safe speed - Flaps up + Max continuous power at 400 ft AGL.
    TECHNIQUE Pre-Stall Approach on Instruments
  • Level flight at altitude. Begin slow descent at small power reduction.
  • Pitch nose up gradually to increase AOA without altitude loss.
  • Monitor airspeed on ASI. Expect buffet or stall warning horn.
  • ON STALL WARNING: Immediate power + pitch correction + go-around.
  • Never approach actual stall (post-stall characteristics) on instruments.

    Simulated GCA (Ground-Controlled Approach)
    Radar-vectored approach simulation — demonstrates workload management
    TECHNIQUE GCA Execution
    Instructor provides ATC-style headings and descent instructions. Student must follow vectors whilst maintaining steady attitude flying. Rate 1 turns, precise altitude keeping. The challenge: manage radio communication and vector-following without degrading instrument scan.
  • Receive vector: "Turn right heading 200."
  • Acknowledge and execute on AI (15° bank).
  • Receive descent instruction: "Descend to 800 feet, maintain safe speed."
  • Set power/pitch for rated descent, maintain vector.
  • Crosscheck altimeter, ASI, VSI periodically.
  • Return to AI immediately after each glance.
    • Workload Management
    GCA tests your ability to handle ATC communication and complex vectoring without losing control. The aircraft flying is ALWAYS the priority. Never let radio work distract from the 90% AI scan rule.
