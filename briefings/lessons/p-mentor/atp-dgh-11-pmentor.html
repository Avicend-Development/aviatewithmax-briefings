How to conduct this first dual lesson after solo — P-Mentor
Flight Post-Solo Assessment & VOR Introduction — P-Mentor G1000

This is the first dual lesson after solo. Assess quickly — check for non-SOP habits from solo flying. If SOP items have been dropped, correct immediately and firmly. General relaxation in standards after solo is common; this lesson re-establishes rigour.

VOR via G1000: "Tune-Select-Ident". On the G1000, tune the KOR VOR frequency on NAV1 (111.6 MHz), select the desired radial on the PFD CDI, listen for Ident via the audio panel. NEVER use a VOR without Ident confirmation. Brief the student: VOR supplements visual navigation — do not navigate by CDI alone. You must navigate just as in VFR — one finger on the map, visual references — and then give the VOR needle a short glance to verify.

Short-field landing (P-Mentor): approach to threshold at 50 ft, touch down in the first third of the runway. Apply maximum braking immediately after touchdown. P-Mentor: nose-wheel-first touchdown acceptable due to tricycle gear — but brief the student to apply firm back pressure to extend the flare slightly and reduce touchdown speed before braking. Maximum braking: full pedal pressure.

Use inbound cruise time productively: obtain ATIS, set up comms, brief the approach, identify the runway in use. Multitasking is a discipline — leaving navigation alone for 30 seconds to complete approach preparation is essential.

SOP P-Mentor: AP limits § 1.3.2; fuel pump ON before approach; no carb heat (fuel-injected) §1.3.2

Common Errors
Anticipate and address these during DGH 11 — P-Mentor
Errors Common Mistakes — DGH 11 P-Mentor
✕Forgetting SOP items after solo — regression to informal techniques, relaxed procedures
✕General relaxation of standards after solo release — "the hard part is done" attitude is a safety risk
✕Forgetting lookout discipline after leaving the circuit environment
✕Over-relying on G1000 VOR CDI — navigating by needle alone instead of visual + VOR crosscheck
✕Omitting VOR Ident step — never acceptable; always confirm station identity before tracking
✕Getting behind the aircraft inbound EKRK — not using available cruise time for approach preparation
✕Short-field: threshold height too high (>50 ft) — resulting in late touchdown past first-third
✕Delayed or insufficient braking after short-field touchdown on P-Mentor

VOR Tune-Select-Ident (G1000) & Short-Field Technique
Briefing VOR Navigation (G1000) & Short-Field Landing — P-Mentor

VOR via G1000 — Tune-Select-Ident: On the G1000, select NAV1 frequency (e.g. KOR 111.6 MHz). Press the CDI softkey to select VOR source. Turn the OBS knob to select the desired radial. Listen for Ident via audio panel — two letter Morse code confirms the station. NEVER track a VOR without Ident confirmation. The CDI shows deviation from the selected radial. VOR is used to supplement visual navigation at this stage.

VOR Key Concept — G1000
Navigate visually — one finger on the map. Give the VOR CDI a short glance to verify position. If you try to navigate on the CDI alone, you will lose situational awareness. Ident is mandatory — always.

Short-field landing (P-Mentor): aim point is the threshold itself; cross at 50 ft AGL. Touch down immediately in the first third. Apply firm back pressure briefly on flare, then full braking. P-Mentor tricycle gear: do not plant the nosewheel hard — brief back pressure reduces nosewheel load during initial braking phase.

Inbound cruise time: use it! ATIS → approach brief → comms setup. Discipline: leave navigation alone for 30 seconds if needed — do not let cruise time pass without completing pre-approach tasks.

SOP P-Mentor: no carb heat (fuel-injected); fuel pump ON before approach; AP OFF below 200 ft §1.3.2 / POH

Checklist Briefing Coverage Checklist — DGH 11 P-Mentor
  • VOR via G1000: Tune-Select-Ident (mandatory); CDI as supplement only

Post-Flight Review
Questions to guide the de-brief discussion
Debrief De-Brief Discussion Points — DGH 11 P-Mentor
1VOR procedure: Was Tune-Select-Ident performed correctly on the G1000? Was Ident confirmed before tracking?
2VOR use: Was VOR used as a supplement to visual navigation, or did the student over-rely on the G1000 CDI?
3Short-field: Was threshold height (~50 ft) achieved? First-third touchdown? Was braking applied promptly and firmly?
4SOP compliance: Have any SOP items been dropped since solo? Did circuit procedures remain standard on P-Mentor?
5Lookout: Was lookout discipline maintained throughout, particularly outside the circuit?
6Approach preparation: Was inbound cruise time used productively (ATIS, comms, brief)?
