EVALUATION: Solo Release Assessment

This is an evaluation lesson ONLY. No instruction, no corrections. Student must demonstrate DGH 00–09 independently. Reassure student: anxiety is normal.

SOLO RELEASE IS WITHHELD IF: Student does not initiate go-around at own initiative when approach is unsatisfactory. Non-negotiable.

Critical Gate: If student fails to initiate go-around when approach is poor, do NOT release. This is the single non-negotiable criterion for P-Mentor solo release.
2
Common Errors
Observe but do not correct during evaluation
ERRORS: Common Mistakes
Not initiating go-around at own initiative — WILL PRECLUDE SOLO RELEASE
Excessive apprehension reducing performance (normal; allow it)
Not applying correct pitch/power corrections (observe only)

BRIEFING: Evaluation Lesson Brief

This is an evaluation lesson only. No new demands. You will fly to DGH 09 standard. I will observe; no guidance. Focus: consistent circuits, independent go-around decisions.

Brief the solo plan: After successful evaluation, your first solo is next. Same procedures, same calls, same circuits. Solo is just the circuit practice you've been doing — but alone.

SOPGo-around: must be YOUR decision § 1.18
2
Key Concepts
What makes a successful solo release
CONCEPT: Independent Go-Around Decision
Non-Negotiable Criterion: You must initiate go-around at your own decision when the approach is unsatisfactory. This decision must be yours.
  • Anxiety on evaluation: completely normal
  • Performance may be lower — that is okay
  • Same circuit standard as DGH 09
  • Go-around decision: yours alone
  • All checks and calls: same as every lesson
CHECKLIST: Briefing Coverage
  • ✓ No new demands — evaluation standard only
  • ✓ Go-around: own initiative, no exception
  • ✓ Anxiety: normal, manageable, expected
  • ✓ Solo plan: same as dual, nothing changes
