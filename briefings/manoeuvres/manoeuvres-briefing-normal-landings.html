05 · Common Mistakes
  • Carrying too much speed onto final — long float, late touchdown beyond first third
  • Flaring too high (above 20 ft) — heavy arrival or ballooning
  • Flaring too late — nose wheel first, three-point bounce possible
  • Looking too close to touchdown point — fixating near runway instead of at far end
  • Not calling "Going around - Full power" promptly when approach unstabilised
  • Forgetting to apply full power smoothly on go-around — insufficient energy
  • Retracting flap directly from 2 to 0 on go-around — sudden lift loss, aircraft settles

01
The Stabilised Approach
Speed · configuration · descent rate · glidepath · decision gates
Key PrincipleStabilisation Rule
Critical Rule
A stabilised approach must be established by 500 ft AGL in VMC. If not stabilised by 500 ft, execute an immediate go-around.
TheoryStabilised Approach Criteria
Element Target Tolerance Decision
Speed 70 KIAS ±5 kt Go around if unstable
Configuration Flap 2 · Gear Down Set abeam threshold Verify before final
Descent Rate 400–600 fpm 3° glidepath nominal Adjust power if excessive
Path (Glidepath) On centreline & glidepath No large corrections Go around if needed

If any element is not stabilised by 500 ft AGL, declare go-around immediately. Executing a go-around is professional airmanship, not a failure.

TechniqueFinal Approach — Speed & Path Control
  1. Maintain 70 KIAS on final — trim to remove control pressure; do not hand-fly speed
  2. Power + pitch together control the glidepath: more power raises descent path; less power lowers it
  3. Observe nose attitude reference — relate nose position to runway threshold for consistency
  4. Aim point technique: hold a point approximately one-third down the runway in the windscreen — if aim point rises, descending shallower (power-off); if it sinks, descending steeper (add power)
  5. As runway threshold passes under the nose, begin transition to flare at 15–20 ft AGL
TechniqueFlare Technique — The Transition to Landing
Key Principle
Round out progressively — do not attempt to hold the aircraft off. Let the speed bleed away and allow the aircraft to settle naturally onto the main wheels.
  1. At approximately 15–20 ft AGL, begin gradual progressive back-pressure on the control column
  2. Keep looking toward the far end of the runway — peripheral vision senses descent rate and height
  3. Reduce power to idle smoothly as flare begins — typically by 200 ft or lower
  4. Aircraft attitude rises progressively — airspeed decays — sinking rate reduces
  5. Maintain small back-pressure as aircraft settles — main wheels contact first
  6. Do NOT force the aircraft onto the runway and do NOT hold it off trying to achieve a perfect landing

02
Rollout and Runway Exit
Post-touchdown · directional control · braking · vacating runway
ProcedureRollout Procedure — After Main Wheel Contact
  1. Maintain light back-pressure after main wheel touchdown to hold nose wheel off as long as practicable
  2. Allow nose wheel to settle gently onto runway — do not force it down
  3. Apply differential braking gently if needed for directional control — rudder remains effective above ~30 kt
  4. Track centreline — use rudder pedal and light differential braking to maintain alignment
  5. Vacate at appropriate taxiway — reduce speed below 30 kt before turning off runway
  6. Select Flap 0 after runway clear — apply parking brake — complete after-landing checklist
03
Go-Around Decision and Execution
Decision criteria · CAPA callout sequence · flap management
Key PrincipleGo-Around Decision Rule
Critical Decision
The go-around decision must be made early and acted on immediately. A late go-around is more dangerous than a firm landing. There is no shame in a go-around — it is professional airmanship.

Execute immediate go-around if:

  • Below 500 ft AGL and NOT stabilised on speed, config, path or descent rate
  • Runway not clear when established short final
  • Windshear encountered on final approach
  • Aircraft not responding normally to control inputs
  • Pilot judgement — "not happy" with the approach
ProcedureCAPA Go-Around Callout Sequence

Verbatim CAPA callout sequence per SOP §1.20. Each call is spoken aloud as the associated action is performed. Power application is prop full forward first, then throttle (right to left — smooth and gentle).

"Going around - Full power" – Initial combined call. Prop lever full forward, then throttle full forward smoothly. Begin rotating nose toward G/A attitude.
"Flaps 1 or less" – Select Flaps 1. Never retract directly from Flap 2 to 0 at low speed/altitude.
"G/A attitude" – Set and verify go-around pitch attitude. Accelerate through Vx 56 KIAS maintaining positive ROC.
"Positive ROC" – Confirm VSI shows positive rate of climb. Aircraft climbing, not descending.
"Safe speed" – Above Vx (56 KIAS). Ready to retract flaps. Do not let nose drop when flaps retract.
"Flaps UP" – Retract flaps fully. Trim back to maintain pitch. Accelerate to Vy 70 KIAS.
"Max continuous power" – At 400 ft AGL, set 2250 RPM MCP. Advise ATC and re-join circuit sequence.
⚠
Critical Flap Management
Never retract flap from Flap 2 to Flap 0 in one movement at low speed and low altitude. The sudden loss of lift causes the aircraft to settle back onto the runway or descend rapidly. Always use intermediate Flap 1 on go-around.

04
Common Scenarios and Decisions
Float · balloon · bounce · wind correction
ScenarioThe Float — Too Much Speed

Cause: Speed carried onto final — typically 75+ KIAS instead of 70 KIAS — aircraft floats down the runway without settling.

Decision: If float extends beyond the first third of the runway, execute go-around immediately. Do NOT attempt to force touchdown in the later two-thirds.

ScenarioThe Balloon — Excessive Back-Pressure

Cause: Excessive back-pressure during flare — aircraft pitches up excessively — lift increases — aircraft climbs away from runway.

Action: Do NOT push forward — this causes nose-wheel-first contact or pitch oscillation (PIO). Apply gentle power to stabilise pitch. If ballooned significantly above runway, go-around.

ScenarioThe Bounce — Firm Touchdown Rebound

Cause: Firm contact on main wheels — aircraft pitches up slightly — tail contacts runway — aircraft becomes airborne again.

Decision: If bounce is significant, go-around immediately. Do NOT attempt to re-land from a bounced aircraft — risk of secondary bounce or damage.

SOPWind and Gust Correction

Gust factor rule: Add half the gust factor to approach speed. Example: METAR reports 15G25 kt = 10 kt gust spread → add 5 kt to final approach → fly 75 KIAS instead of 70 KIAS.

Brief instructor: Always report wind conditions and confirm adjusted approach speed with instructor before committing to final approach.

05
Debrief Checklist
Post-flight review of landing performance
DebriefLanding Performance Checklist — 8 Items
1
Was final approach stabilised by 500 ft AGL on all landings?
2
Was approach speed maintained at 70 KIAS ±5 kt throughout final?
3
Was flare initiated at correct height (15–20 ft) — not too high, not too late?
4
Were touchdowns on main wheels first, within first third of runway, on centreline?
5
Was rollout controlled — directional tracking maintained to runway exit?
6
Were go-arounds executed with the full CAPA callout sequence: "Going around - Full power - Flaps 1 or less - G/A attitude - Positive ROC - Safe speed - Flaps UP" then "Max continuous power"?
7
Was prop full forward applied before throttle (right to left)? Were flaps retracted through Flap 1 — never directly 2 to 0?
8
Was radio communication clear and timely throughout circuit operations?
