02 · Student Prerequisites
  • Completed standard circuit training at EKRK — can fly a full circuit independently
  • Familiar with normal approach and landing procedure (SOP §1.18–1.19)
  • Can operate radio: R/T calls for downwind, base, final without prompting
  • Understands the Visual Flight Guide (VFG) and can extract aerodrome data
  • Reviewed CAPA SOP §1.17.1 uncontrolled aerodrome procedures prior to briefing
03 · Lesson Objectives
  • Join the circuit via the overhead join: arrive at 1500 ft AGL, assess runway and wind, descend dead side to 1000 ft AGL
  • Select the active runway based on windsock and visual signals
  • Broadcast correct blind position reports at each circuit position
  • Maintain traffic awareness throughout — self-separate without ATC
  • Make a go-around decision independently when approach is not stabilised
  • Read and respond to visual ground signals (light signals / signal square)

05 · Motivation

The majority of VFR cross-country destinations in Denmark and northern Europe are uncontrolled aerodromes. Without ATC to issue instructions, sequence traffic, or provide separation, the arriving pilot is solely responsible for assessing the circuit, selecting the runway, detecting other traffic, and self-separating. An incorrect overhead join, missed radio call, or failure to see conflicting traffic at an uncontrolled field can lead to a collision with no warning. The skills in this lesson are not supplementary — they are essential for safe solo cross-country flight.

06 · Common Mistakes
  • Arriving below 1500 ft AGL for the overhead — windsock not visible, circuit assessment incomplete
  • Circling over the signal square — reduces own view of windsock and blocks other traffic's view
  • Flying the dead side and downwind too close to the runway — other traffic cannot see you
  • Going below 1000 ft AGL in the circuit before established on final
  • Missing or garbled radio calls — no position reports at key positions
  • Not scanning for traffic on base and final before turning
  • Committing to land on an unstabilised approach — no go-around decision made

01
Environment
Uncontrolled vs. controlled · AFIS vs. UNICOM · VFG data · pre-arrival planning
Theory Uncontrolled Aerodrome — Types of Service & VFG Prep
▸ Types of Service at Uncontrolled Fields
  • AFIS (Aerodrome Flight Information Service): Operator provides traffic info and weather. Non-binding — pilot still makes own decisions. Mandatory to call inbound.
  • UNICOM / CTAF: Self-announce on published frequency. No operator. All pilots broadcast position reports. Listen before transmitting.
  • No radio service: Observe signal square and windsock only. Standard circuit altitudes apply. Check VFG for procedures.
  • Always check VFG for: frequency, hours of service, runway lighting, known glider/parachute activity
SOP Check VFG for local restrictions and AFIS hours before departure — not on arrival § 1.17.1
▸ Pre-Arrival Planning (Before Departure)
  • Extract from VFG: aerodrome elevation, RWY headings, AFIS/UNICOM frequency
  • Check published circuit direction (left or right hand) — not all runways use left-hand circuit
  • Note any NOTAM: closed runway, glider activity, parachute ops — check before departure
  • Calculate joining altitude: field elevation + 1500 ft AGL = overhead joining altitude MSL
  • Set altitude alerter to overhead join altitude on descent to destination
  • Brief student before arrival: "At [field], we will join overhead at [altitude] MSL. AFIS on [freq]. Expected RWY based on TAF wind: [XX]."
02
Setup
Initial call · overhead arrival · windsock assessment · runway selection
Procedure Inbound Call & Overhead Join Setup (SOP §1.17.1)
Key Principle
Arrive at 1500 ft AGL. Do not descend below that until the circuit assessment is complete and you have chosen the active runway. Never circle directly over the signal area — this blocks your view of the windsock and prevents other traffic from seeing your position.
▸ Inbound Call (10–15 NM Out)
  1. Tune and identify AFIS/UNICOM frequency. Listen before transmitting.
  2. Transmit: "[Field] Radio, OY-XXX, [type], [position], [altitude], inbound, request joining information"
  3. Note AFIS advisory: QNH, active RWY, known traffic, wind.
  4. Set QNH from AFIS/ATIS. Recalculate joining altitude in MSL if field elevation requires.
▸ Overhead Arrival Assessment
  1. Arrive overhead at 1500 ft AGL on the non-traffic side (dead side) — do not overfly the runway in use.
  2. Position aircraft for a clear view of the windsock and signal square. Check: wind direction, RWY in use, visual signals (light gun / signal square markings).
  3. Confirm active RWY. Assess traffic in the pattern — identify any aircraft on downwind, base, or final.
  4. Call: "[Field], OY-XXX, overhead, [altitude], joining [circuit direction] for RWY [XX]"
FI Note
Point out the windsock before the student arrives overhead — use it as a teaching moment on wind assessment. Ask: "Which RWY would you select and why?" Ensure the student is looking at the signal square and not just assuming the expected RWY is in use. If glider/parachute activity is suspected — orbit at 1500 ft until confirmed safe to join.

03
Execution
Dead-side descent · circuit join · downwind · base · final · go-around
Procedure Overhead Join & Circuit (SOP §1.17.1 / §1.18)
▸ Dead Side Descent to 1000 ft AGL
  1. From overhead, track to the dead side of the runway (side opposite normal downwind leg). Do not overfly the active runway.
  2. Begin descent from 1500 ft AGL to 1000 ft AGL on the dead side. Reduce power — set 2250 RPM, maintain 90 KIAS.
  3. At the upwind end of the runway (threshold end), cross overhead at 1000 ft AGL and join the downwind leg.
  4. Scan for traffic entering the downwind from base/final before joining. Look out actively — responsibility is yours.
▸ Circuit — Downwind to Final
  1. Downwind: Call "[Field], OY-XXX, downwind RWY [XX]". Maintain 1000 ft AGL. Abeam threshold: set 12" MAP, check speed, flaps 1.
  2. Base: Scan final for traffic before turning. Call "[Field], OY-XXX, base RWY [XX]". Flaps 2 at 70 KIAS.
  3. Final: Call "[Field], OY-XXX, final RWY [XX]". Complete final checks. Final turn must be completed by 500 ft AGL. Target 63 KIAS at threshold.
  4. Landing: Maintain centreline. On flare, reduce power to idle. Hold off. Touch down on main gear, directional control with rudder.
⛔
Go-Around Decision Criteria
Go around immediately if: approach is unstabilised at 300 ft AGL (speed, glidepath, or alignment not correct) · unexpected traffic on the runway · landing area obstructed · wind shift causes excessive correction requirement · any doubt whatsoever. Radio call after procedure: "[Field], OY-XXX, going around RWY [XX]". Aviate first — communicate after.
SOP §1.20 "Going around - Full power - Flaps 1 or less - G/A attitude - Positive ROC - Safe speed - Flaps UP" → "Max continuous power" (400 ft AGL). Prop full forward first, then throttle. §1.20
04
Check
Assessment criteria · debrief questions · knowledge check · progression
Standards Performance Standards & Debrief
▸ Minimum Acceptable Performance
  • Arrives overhead at 1500 ft AGL — not below
  • Correctly reads windsock and selects appropriate runway
  • Makes all mandatory radio calls without prompting
  • Joins circuit from dead side at 1000 ft AGL
  • Scans for traffic before each turn: downwind, base, final
  • Makes independent go-around decision if approach is not stable
▸ Key Debrief Questions
1
What did the windsock tell you on the overhead? How did you determine the active runway?
2
Where is the "dead side"? Why do we descend on it rather than on the traffic side?
3
At which circuit positions did you scan for traffic? What were you looking for on base before turning final?
4
What is the difference between AFIS advisory information and an ATC clearance?
Review End-of-Lesson Knowledge Check
  • At what altitude AGL do you arrive overhead an uncontrolled aerodrome?
  • What is the "dead side" of a runway?
  • What altitude do you join the downwind leg at, and when?
  • At what altitude must the final turn be completed?
  • What is the difference between AFIS and UNICOM?
  • Name three mandatory radio call positions in the circuit.
  • List five go-around criteria for an uncontrolled aerodrome.
  • Why should you NOT circle over the signal square?
