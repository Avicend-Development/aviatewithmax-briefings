05 · Motivation

The take-off is the transition from ground to air — the most critical phase of every flight. Correct line-up procedure, departure briefing, and an accurate take-off roll are foundational skills that every subsequent exercise depends on. The procedures taught here are identical in structure to those used throughout the CAPA programme and must be executed precisely and consistently from the very first exposure. Errors in this phase carry the highest consequence of any training exercise.

06 · Common Mistakes
  • Insufficient rudder on take-off roll — aircraft yaws left uncorrected
  • Missing or incorrect departure briefing — incomplete engine failure plan
  • Forgetting HSI check on line-up ("Heading indicator checked — RWY XX identified")
  • Not calling "Final clear" before entering RWY
  • Rotating too slowly — aircraft floats instead of climbing positively
  • Forgetting speed call before flap retraction at 400 ft AGL
  • Drifting off extended centreline on climb-out — not tracking reference point
  • Forgetting to set MCP 2250 RPM after flap retraction

Environment
Operating conditions · circuit area · weather minima · ATC
Theory Operating Environment & Conditions
  • Location: EKRK circuit — stay within CTR for circuit exercises
  • Altitude: Circuit at 1000 ft AGL / 1150 ft MSL (EKRK elevation 150 ft)
  • Weather minima: VMC, cloudbase min 1500 ft, vis min 5 km
  • Wind: Calm to light preferred. XWC max 5 kt for initial t/o training
  • Turbulence: Light or less. If gusty: add ½ gust factor to VR (max +10 kt)
  • EFB: Student min 70% battery, VFG updated (§ 0.6.1)

  • Freq: Roskilde TWR 118.905 (COM 1) / 119.655 (standby)
  • ATIS: COM 2 standby — receive before start, set QNH
  • Squawk: 7000 VFR
  • QNH: Set both altimeters — verify ≤90 ft difference, elev +/−60 ft
  • ATIS considerations: RWY in use, XWC, SVFR conditions

02
Setup
Departure briefing · run-up area · line-up checklist · pre-take-off
Procedure Departure Briefing & Line-Up Sequence
Key Principle
Never report ready for departure unless mentally prepared, checklists complete, and engine failure plan fully briefed. The departure briefing is a hard requirement — not optional.
▸ Run-Up Area — Departure Briefing (§ 1.9.1)
  1. Complete BEFORE TAKE-OFF checklist (READ & DO). Complete LINE-UP checklist.
  2. Set HDG bug to Wind Corrected Heading (WCH) to track extended centreline.
  3. Set altitude warning to planned level-off altitude (e.g. 1300 ft MSL).
  4. Deliver departure briefing aloud: "VFR departure RWY [XX], Roskilde. Normal take-off. After departure climb straight ahead 650 ft MSL (500 ft AGL), expect [turn direction] climbing [altitude]. HDG bug and altitude warning set. Before VR — WE STOP. After VR below 650 ft — nose down, 70 KIAS, throttle idle, land straight ahead within 45°, preferably to [direction]. Above 650 ft — standard emergency."
  5. Verify Pitch Trim Selector set to LH (student side).
▸ Line-Up (§ 1.10)
  1. Check ATC clearance received. Scan final for traffic.
  2. Call out: "Final clear — Lining up RWY [XX]"
  3. Enter RWY. On uncontrolled: scan base and final before entering.
  4. Check HSI — verify on correct RWY heading. Call out: "Heading indicator checked — RWY [XX] identified"
  5. Select reference point on extended centreline for climb-out tracking.
SOP Wake turbulence: start timer at rotation of preceding traffic. Twin Otter = medium — wait ~90 sec § 1.10

03
Execution
Normal take-off roll · rotation · climb-out · flap retraction · MCP
Theory Take-Off Aerodynamics & Control
  • Engine torque and P-factor yaw the nose left on full power — right rudder required throughout the roll
  • Slight forward pressure on control wheel prevents premature rotation and keeps nose wheel on surface for directional control
  • At VR the wings generate sufficient lift to fly — rotation must be prompt and positive to 70 KIAS attitude
  • Flap 1 provides extra lift for shorter ground roll and lower VR
  • VX 56 KIAS = best angle (obstacle clearance) · VY 70 KIAS = best rate (normal climb)
  • MCP 2250 RPM set after flap retraction — engine protection and efficient climb
Manoeuvre Normal Take-Off — P-Mentor (SOP § 1.11.1)
Key Principle
Power on — directional control with rudder — let the aircraft fly itself off. Rotate promptly at 53 KIAS. Think attitude and aircraft movement, never control input.
Phase 1 · Take-Off Roll
  1. ATC cleared for take-off: switch LANDING light ON, maintain/select TAXI light ON.
  2. Hold brakes. Advance throttle smoothly to MAP 20". Call out: "Engine instruments checked — Timing started"
  3. Release brakes. Advance throttle smoothly to full power.
  4. Call out: "Full power" — verify RPM and MAP at full power.
  5. Apply right rudder — maintain centreline. Slight forward pressure on control wheel. Track reference point.
Phase 2 · Rotation & Initial Climb
  1. At 53 KIAS: Call out "53 — Rotate" — release forward pressure and let aircraft accelerate to 70 KIAS.
  2. Establish and hold VY 70 KIAS climb attitude. Trim to relieve pressure.
  3. Track extended centreline — use reference point selected at line-up.
Phase 3 · Flap Retraction & Climb-Out
  1. At 400 ft AGL: check positive ROC, verify safe speed (min VX 56 KIAS). Call out: "Safe speed — Flaps up"
  2. Select flaps UP. Raise nose slightly to maintain VY 70 KIAS. Retard propeller to 2250 RPM (MCP). Call out: "Max continuous power". Re-trim.
  3. At 500 ft AGL: lookout, commence turn as briefed.
  4. Passing 1000 ft AGL: lower pitch slightly, trim for cruise climb 80 KIAS. Select TAXI light OFF. Complete AFTER TAKE-OFF checklist from memory.
SOP No checklist reading below 1000 ft AGL except memory items (flaps) § 1.11.1
SOP Turbulence / gusts: delay VR by 5 kt (or ½ gust factor). Climb at 70 KIAS flaps 1, then VY+5 after flap retraction § 1.11.1 NOTE

⛔
Warning — Engine Failure After VR
Below 650 ft MSL (500 ft AGL): nose down, 70 KIAS, throttle idle, land straight ahead within 45° (into wind). Do NOT attempt to return to the field. Above 650 ft: standard emergency procedures. This must be briefed BEFORE every take-off.
⚠
Caution — Flap Retraction
ALWAYS verify safe speed (min VX 56 KIAS) and call out before selecting flaps. Never retract flaps if airspeed is insufficient — aircraft will sink. If positive ROC not confirmed, do not retract.
Common Errors — Take-Off Execution
  • Insufficient right rudder — nose drifts left on power application
  • Late rotation — aircraft skips or floats on ground
  • Not maintaining 70 KIAS climb attitude — climbing at wrong speed
  • Missing speed call before flaps up at 400 ft AGL
  • Not tracking centreline after lift-off — drifting in circuit
  • Forgetting MCP 2250 RPM after flap retraction
FI Teaching Points
Demo the full sequence on first attempt — talk through all steps aloud. Student takes over on 2nd take-off. Watch for feet on brakes during roll — common early error. Ensure student calls out each step; silence = not thinking. If student drifts off centreline, ask them to identify their reference point. Praise positive procedure language: "Rotate" not "pull back".

Assessment Lesson Assessment Criteria
✓ Satisfactory — Progress
  • Departure briefing complete and operationally correct
  • Line-up calls made correctly at each stage
  • Centreline maintained during take-off roll (±5 m)
  • VR call-out at 53 KIAS with prompt rotation
  • 70 KIAS climb attitude established and maintained
  • Correct speed call before flap retraction at 400 ft AGL
  • MCP set and centreline tracked on climb-out
✕ Unsatisfactory — Repeat
  • Departure briefing missing or engine failure plan incomplete
  • No "Final clear" call before RWY entry
  • No HSI check on line-up
  • Consistent directional control failures on roll
  • Flap retraction without speed call or at unsafe speed
  • No VR call-out, or late rotation
