05 · Motivation

The 30° medium turn is the foundation of practical flying. At 30° bank, load factor rises slightly to 1.15 g, yet stall speed increases only by 7% — remaining well within the safety margins of normal flight. This lesson teaches precise control, smooth coordination, and the discipline of using lead points for accurate roll-out. Students who master medium turns develop the muscle memory and scan habits needed for all subsequent advanced manoeuvres.

06 · Common Mistakes
  • Ball not centred — insufficient rudder during roll-in or roll-out
  • Altitude loss — not applying back pressure as bank increases
  • Rolling out too late or too early — missing lead point by more than 10°
  • Fixating on bank angle — forgetting to scan altitude and airspeed
  • Uncoordinated entry — using rudder to correct bank angle instead of aileron
  • Over-banking tendency ignored — allowing bank to exceed 35°
  • No external reference — relying only on instruments instead of horizon

01
Load Factor & Coordination
Aerodynamic load · stall speed · control coordination · rudder technique
Theory Load Factor at 30° Bank
▸ Load Factor Calculation
  • Load factor (n) = 1 / cos(bank angle)
  • At 30° bank: n = 1 / cos(30°) = 1.15 g
  • Only a slight increase over level flight (1.0 g)
  • Stall speed increases: VS(turn) = VS(level) × √n
  • At 30° bank: stall speed ≈ VS × 1.07 (approximately 7% increase)
  • P-Mentor VS ≈ 44 KIAS → 30° turn stall speed ≈ 47 KIAS — plenty of safety margin
SOP At 30° bank, stall speed increases modestly. Maintain normal cruise airspeed throughout. § 1.22
▸ Coordination Principle
  • Coordination: ball centred at all times — no skid or slip
  • Ball shows rate of turn vs. bank angle — centred = coordinated
  • In left turn: left aileron creates adverse yaw — apply left rudder to coordinate
  • Right turn: right aileron → right rudder required
  • Secondary effect of ailerons: lead with rudder as aileron applied
  • Ball displacement indicates need for more/less rudder — continuous adjustment
SOP Ball is the primary reference for coordination — keep it centred using rudder as the primary control. § 1.3
02
Roll-In Technique
Entry · coordinated aileron & rudder · back pressure · trim
Procedure Entering a 30° Coordinated Turn
  • Check CLEAR of traffic — look in the direction of intended turn for a full 360° horizon view
  • Apply coordinated aileron and rudder in direction of turn smoothly
  • As bank reaches 30°, apply slight back pressure on yoke to maintain altitude
  • Bank rate should be smooth — approximately 10–15° per second
  • Once established at 30°, hold bank angle using visual reference (wingtip to horizon)
  • Trim slightly if needed — avoid fighting the controls
  • Scan continuously: AI (bank) → ALT → VSI → Airspeed → Horizon → repeat
    • SOP Smooth entry is essential. Coordinated aileron + rudder + back pressure must work together — not sequentially. § 1.22

    03
    Maintaining & Roll-Out
    Sustained turn · lead point · roll-out technique · left vs. right turns
    Technique Maintaining a 30° Banked Turn
    ▸ Holding Bank Angle
    • Primary reference: horizon-to-wingtip relationship (outer wing pointing below horizon)
    • Secondary reference: AI — bank angle indicator must show 30°
    • Back pressure: maintained (not increasing) — trim can assist
    • Visual check: nose traces smooth arc on horizon
    • Instrument cross-check cycle: AI (bank) → ALT → VSI → AI — frequent and rhythmic
    ▸ Monitoring Coordination
    • Ball: continuously monitored — any skid or slip requires rudder correction
    • Skid (ball moves inside): apply rudder opposite to turn direction
    • Slip (ball moves outside): apply rudder in direction of turn
    • Correction should be small and smooth — not abrupt
    • Airspeed: watch for decay — if IAS drops, reduce back pressure slightly
    SOP Scan: horizon → bank angle → altitude → airspeed → ball — repeat continuously. No fixation. § 1.22
    Technique Roll-Out Using Lead Point
  • Lead point = half the bank angle = 15° before desired exit heading
  • Example: turning to North (360°) from South, rolling right — begin roll-out at 015° on heading indicator
  • Apply coordinated aileron and rudder opposite to turn direction — smooth and progressive
  • Release back pressure gradually as wings level — do not dump the nose
  • Roll-out rate should mirror roll-in rate — approximately 10–15° per second
  • As wings reach level, reduce power if it was increased, and retrim for level flight
    • SOP Roll-out lead equals half the bank angle for medium turns (30°). This is a standard reference — practise until instinctive. § 1.22
    Technique Left vs. Right Turn Differences
    • ROTAX 912 Torque Effect: Engine produces slight left-turning tendency at power
    • Right turn: Adverse yaw effect more pronounced — may require slightly more right rudder on entry than left rudder in left turn
    • Left turn: P-factor and torque assist the turn — may require less left rudder on entry
    • Both turns: Ball is the reference — keep it centred regardless of asymmetries
    • In all cases, use ball displacement to adjust rudder — do not attempt to memorise differences
    ⛔
    Traffic Lookout
    Before entering any turn, perform clearing turn check in the direction of intended turn. Look left if turning left; look right if turning right. Scan above and below horizon. No exceptions.
    04
    Debrief Criteria
    Assessment · progression decision · knowledge questions
    Debrief Structured Debrief Questions
    1
    Was the 30° bank maintained ±5° throughout both turns (left and right)? What technique did you use to hold bank angle?
    2
    Was the ball centred at all times? If not, what control input did you use to correct skid/slip?
    3
    Was altitude maintained ±100 ft? If there was altitude loss or gain, what would you adjust?
    4
    Did you use the lead point correctly? How many degrees of lead did you apply, and did you exit within ±10° of target heading?
    5
    Rate your scan discipline: did you fixate on any one instrument, or did you maintain a smooth cross-check throughout?
    6
    What differences did you notice between the left and right turns? Which felt more coordinated?
