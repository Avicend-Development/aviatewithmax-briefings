02 · Prerequisites
  • Completed pre-flight inspection — airframe, engine, controls checked
  • Cockpit familiarisation — location of all switches, instruments, circuit breakers known
  • ATC radio phraseology — understands readback requirements and standard calls
  • Traffic pattern awareness — understands local aerodrome layout
03 · Learning Objectives
  • Obtain correct ATC clearance before moving — read back runway, taxiway, hold short instructions
  • Taxi at safe speed with differential braking and appropriate wing attitude for crosswind
  • Conduct engine run-up per CAPA SOP checklist — verify LANE A/B integrity, all systems green
  • Identify defects that prevent flight — take no-go decisions with confidence
  • Complete pre-take-off checks (BUMFICH) correctly before entering runway

05 · Common Mistakes
  • Not checking for traffic on taxiway — always visual scan before entering
  • Taxiing too fast — braking distance insufficient in tight turns
  • Forgetting to set parking brake before applying run-up power
  • Not monitoring oil temperature — power application before >50°C causes engine stress
  • Incomplete BUMFICH — missing items are hidden defects waiting to happen in flight

1
Taxi Procedures
ATC clearance · taxiway navigation · crosswind technique · runway crossings
SOP Obtaining Clearance & Initial Taxi

Cleared to taxi:

  • Read back runway, taxiway routing, and hold-short instructions — verify ATC acknowledges
  • Check instruments once more: heading indicator correct, lights on, landing gear check
  • Release parking brake and apply slow, controlled power — walking pace only (~5 knots)
  • In congested areas or narrow taxiways, reduce speed further — aim for minimal thrust needed
SOP E2R1 §1.4 Always obtain explicit ATC clearance before entering any runway. Read back all runway crossing/take-off clearances.
TECHNIQUE En Route — Turns & Attitude
  • Use differential braking for turns — NOT excessive speed into corners
  • Apply opposing brake gently; coordinate with nose wheel steering (if equipped)
  • When tail/nose attitude blocks forward view: execute shallow S-turns to see ahead
  • Crosswind taxi technique: aileron into wind (upwind aileron UP) — prevents weathercocking
  • Wings level in calm conditions; maintain the crosswind correction throughout taxi
  • Stop-and-look at all runway crossings — even with explicit ATC clearance, visually verify clear

Pre-Run-Up Checks
Run-up area positioning · systems verification · temperature limits
CHECKS Run-Up Position & System Status
  • Position aircraft into wind, clear of other aircraft, clear of prop blast hazards
  • Set parking brake firmly — hold brakes throughout run-up; do not rely on friction lock alone
  • Oil temperature: must be >50°C before run-up power is applied (ROTAX requirement)
  • Oil pressure: confirm 2.0–5.0 bar (normal range on gauge)
  • Fuel selection: select both tanks; use fullest tank on selector if available
  • Fuel pressure: confirm 2.8–3.2 bar within specification
  • Throttle friction: adjust to prevent creep during climb — set resistance appropriately
  • Flight controls: cycle pitch, roll, yaw fully and freely — confirm no stiffness or binding (EFATO consideration)
3
Engine Run-Up — ROTAX 912 iSc 3
Fuel-injected engine · dual electronic ignition (LANE A/B) · health checks
IMPORTANT NOTE
The ROTAX 912 iSc 3 is fuel-injected — no carburettor heat check applies. It uses dual electronic ignition (LANE A and LANE B) in place of magnetos. Ignition system switches are your primary electrical systems check during run-up.
CHECKS ROTAX 912 iSc 3 Run-Up Sequence
  • Set 3000 RPM: hold brakes firmly with both feet, advance throttle to 3000 RPM (baseline)
  • LANE A check: switch ignition to LANE A only — observe RPM drop. Maximum acceptable drop: 50 RPM
  • Switch to BOTH LANES — confirm RPM returns to 3000 RPM baseline
  • LANE B check: from BOTH, switch ignition to LANE B only — observe RPM drop. Maximum acceptable drop: 50 RPM
  • Return to BOTH LANES — confirm stable 3000 RPM
  • Throttle response check: advance smoothly from 3000 RPM to 2250 RPM (MCP setting) — check for immediate response, no hesitation or lag
  • Return to idle (1000–1100 RPM target) — confirm minimum stable idle of 1400 RPM after warm-up
  • EGT and engine temperatures: all parameters in green range — no red-line exceedances
⚠
ROTAX 912 iSc 3 LANE CHECK LIMITS
Maximum acceptable RPM drop on single-lane operation is 50 RPM. If drop exceeds 50 RPM or engine runs rough on either lane — DO NOT DEPART. Report defect to maintenance immediately.

Pre-Take-Off Checks (BUMFICH)
Final configuration · systems armed · ready to enter runway
CHECKS BUMFICH Mnemonic — Final Pre-Departure
  • Brakes — set during check, release only when ready to enter runway
  • Undercarriage — fixed gear confirmed (visual check, no retract mechanism available)
  • Mixture/Fuel — fuel sufficient for flight + reserve, correct tank selected, pressure normal
  • Flaps — set for take-off (Flap 1 per CAPA SOP — typically 5–10° depending on AFM)
  • Instruments — altimeter set to QNH, directional indicator aligned to magnetic compass, artificial horizon erect
  • Compass — swing check against known heading; confirm compass and DI are aligned
  • Hatches/Harness — canopy latched and locked, all harnesses tight and locked, doors secure
SOP E2R1 §1.5 T/O config: Flap 1 · MCP (2250 RPM) to 500 ft AGL minimum before any power reduction.
5
Debrief & Self-Assessment
Evaluation criteria · learning review · next steps
DEBRIEF Debrief Criteria & Performance Review
1
Was ATC clearance obtained and read back correctly before moving? Did you verify all hold-short and routing instructions?
2
Was taxi speed appropriate (walking pace)? Did you avoid excessive power and unnecessary acceleration?
3
Was run-up position correct (into wind, clear of hazards)? Did you set parking brake firmly?
4
Was oil temperature verified >50°C before applying run-up power? Why is this critical for ROTAX engines?
5
Were LANE A/B checks conducted correctly? Did you understand what RPM drop >50 RPM would indicate?
6
Were all BUMFICH items completed before entering runway? Did you confirm every switch and system?
7
Any defects identified during run-up or checks? How would you handle a failed system check?
🛑
SCHEDULE PRESSURE IS NOT AN EXCUSE
NEVER RUSH PRE-DEPARTURE CHECKS. No schedule pressure, no waiting passengers, no delays justify skipping any item on the checklist. If in doubt about any system: stay on the ground. A 30-minute ground delay is better than a 30-minute search-and-rescue callout.
CORE PRINCIPLE
Thorough ground operations set the foundation for safe flight. Every checklist item exists because something went wrong once. Respect the procedures.
