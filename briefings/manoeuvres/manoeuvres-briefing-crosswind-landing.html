05 · Motivation

Crosswind landings are among the most frequently demanded skills in everyday VFR operations. Weather rarely provides a perfect headwind. The CAPA de-crab technique — crab on final, de-crab in the flare — produces safe, consistent centreline touchdowns while minimising sideward load on the undercarriage. Mastering this technique is a prerequisite for solo and all future circuit exercises in variable wind conditions.

06 · Common Mistakes
  • De-crabbing too early — drifting off centreline before flare
  • De-crabbing too late — touching down in a crab (side load on undercarriage)
  • Applying rudder only — not applying simultaneous aileron into wind
  • Over-banking into wind — allowing aircraft to slip toward upwind side
  • Relaxing crosswind controls after touchdown — aircraft skids sideways
  • Not adding +5 kt to threshold speed in strong XWC (per AFM requirement)
  • Approaching below 2° glidepath — 4 reds on PAPI (hard limit)
  • Flaring too high in crosswind — airspeed bleed leads to heavy touchdown

Hard Limits — Crosswind Operations
Never exceed maximum XWC per AFM. Add +5 kt to approach and threshold speed per AFM in strong XWC. In gusts: add ½ gust factor (max +10 kt). Never approach below 2° (4 red PAPI). Go around if any doubt about approach quality or centreline control.

02
Setup
Circuit configuration · downwind · base · final speed schedule
Procedure Circuit Configuration & Final Speed Schedule — P-Mentor
Key Principle
All normal circuit speeds and configuration apply. Add +5 kt to approach and threshold speed in strong XWC (per AFM). Maintain wind-corrected heading on all legs to fly a rectangular ground track.
▸ Downwind (§ 1.18.1)
  1. Set (verify) propeller 2250 RPM, MAP 22–23", speed 90 KIAS. Maintain 1000 ft AGL.
  2. Apply WCA on downwind leg to track parallel to RWY. HDG bug on WCH.
  3. Abeam threshold: set MAP 12", call out: "Speed [XX] kt checked — Flaps 1"
  4. Maintain altitude initially. Descend as speed reduces to 80 KIAS.
  5. Call out: "Flaps 1 set" — verify flaps 1 on PFD.
▸ Base
  1. Check for traffic on final and after turning base. Call out: "Final clear"
  2. Apply wind correction on base to avoid drifting into/away from final.
▸ Final (§ 1.18.1 + 1.18.5)
  1. Final turn complete not later than 500 ft AGL.
  2. Check speed still 80 KIAS. Select flaps 2. Set propeller full forward. Call out: "Final check: Propeller fine — Flaps 2 set"
  3. Establish crab — WCA to maintain extended centreline ground track at 70 KIAS (+ XWC addition if applicable).
  4. Monitor PAPI: target 2 white / 2 red. Adjust power for glidepath. Adjust pitch for speed.
  5. Short final: bleed off speed to 63 KIAS (+XWC addition) at threshold.

03
Execution
Crab on final · de-crab in flare · wing-low touchdown · roll-out control
Theory Crosswind Landing — Aerodynamics & CAPA Method
  • Crab angle: WCA applied to maintain centreline ground track. Nose points into wind, ground track follows extended centreline
  • De-crab: At flare — simultaneous rudder (align nose with RWY) + aileron into wind (lower upwind wing). Counteract each other to maintain centreline
  • Wing-low touchdown: Upwind main wheel touches first, absorbing sideward energy. Then downwind main wheel, then nosewheel
  • Control after T/D: Maintain aileron into wind and rudder for directional control throughout roll-out. Adjust as speed decreases
  • High XWC: Flare less than normal to maintain positive control until touchdown. Reduces float and preserves aileron authority
  • Drift fix: Drifting right = bank less. Drifting left = bank more. Nose right = less rudder. Nose left = more rudder
Manoeuvre Crosswind Landing — De-Crab During Flare (CAPA Method · § 1.18.5)
Key Principle
Crab all the way to the flare — maintain centreline with WCA. At flare: rudder aligns the nose + aileron into wind lowers the upwind wing. Two inputs simultaneously — they must exactly counteract each other.
Phase 1 · Final Approach — Crab
  1. Establish crab angle (WCA) on final to track extended centreline. HDG bug on WCH.
  2. Maintain 70 KIAS (+5 kt XWC addition per AFM if applicable). Power governs glidepath.
  3. Monitor PAPI: 2W/2R. Never below 4 red (minimum 2° approach angle).
  4. Hold crab throughout approach to threshold — do NOT de-crab early.
Phase 2 · Flare — De-Crab
  1. Reduce throttle slowly passing threshold. Continue to reduce to idle in flare.
  2. Enter flare at normal height. At flare initiation — simultaneously:
  3. Apply rudder (into wind side) to align nose straight down the RWY.
  4. Apply aileron (into wind) to lower upwind wing — counteract drift toward centreline.
  5. The two inputs must exactly counteract. If drifting right → less aileron. If drifting left → more aileron.
  6. Throttle to idle before touchdown.
Phase 3 · Touchdown & Roll-Out
  1. Touch down on upwind main wheel first. Then downwind main wheel. Then nosewheel.
  2. Maintain crosswind control inputs (aileron into wind + rudder for directional control) throughout roll-out — do NOT relax!
  3. Adjust rudder after touchdown to maintain centreline position. Adjust aileron as speed decreases (increase input as airspeed drops).
  4. Apply brakes as required to slow. Vacate RWY at normal taxi speed.

⛔
Warning — Maintain Controls After Touchdown
Do NOT relax crosswind control inputs after touchdown. Without sustained aileron into wind and rudder for directional control, the aircraft will skid or weathervane sideways on the runway. As speed decreases, progressively increase aileron deflection to compensate for reducing aerodynamic effectiveness.
⚠
Caution — Timing of De-Crab
De-crabbing too early results in drift off centreline before touchdown. Too late and the aircraft lands in a crab, imposing sideward load on the undercarriage. De-crab must happen in the flare — not before, not at touchdown.
Common Errors — Crosswind Landing Execution
  • De-crab before flare — drifts off centreline
  • Rudder only, no aileron — aircraft drifts downwind
  • Over-bank into wind — slipping toward upwind
  • Relaxing inputs after touchdown — skid or weathervane
  • Missing +5 kt speed addition in strong XWC
  • Below 2° glidepath — 4 red PAPI (never acceptable)
  • Flaring too high — float, heavy touchdown
  • Not going around when approach is unstabilised
FI Teaching Points
Demo the full sequence on the first approach. On student attempts, prompt them to hold crab until flare is initiated — common instinct is to de-crab early. Verbalise all inputs: "Rudder — Aileron — simultaneously." After touchdown, watch for student relaxing controls — immediately prompt: "Keep aileron in. Keep rudder." If centreline is lost on final, go around — do not attempt to salvage a poor approach in crosswind. After each circuit, debrief the de-crab timing specifically.

Warning Go-Around — Mandatory Triggers in Crosswind
  • Approach unstabilised below 500 ft AGL (speed, glidepath, or centreline not within tolerance)
  • 4 red PAPI — approach below 2° glidepath at any point on final
  • Aircraft drifting off centreline on short final with insufficient control authority remaining
  • Any doubt about landing outcome — go around immediately without hesitation
SOP Go-Around: "Going around — Full power — Flaps 1 or less — G/A attitude — Positive ROC — Safe speed — Flaps UP — Max continuous power" § 1.20
Assessment Lesson Assessment Criteria
✓ Satisfactory — Progress
  • XWC calculated and confirmed within limits before flight
  • Crab maintained on final until flare — centreline held
  • De-crab executed at correct moment (in flare, not before)
  • Rudder + aileron applied simultaneously with correct magnitude
  • Touchdown on upwind main wheel on centreline (±5 m)
  • XWC inputs maintained through roll-out
  • Correct speed additions applied (+5 kt if required by AFM)
  • Go-around executed correctly when initiated
✕ Unsatisfactory — Repeat
  • De-crab executed consistently at wrong timing
  • Persistent drift off centreline on final or touchdown
  • No aileron applied — rudder only technique
  • Controls relaxed after touchdown — loss of directional control
  • 4 red PAPI on final without go-around initiated
  • Speed additions not applied in strong XWC
  • Unable to self-correct centreline deviations on final
