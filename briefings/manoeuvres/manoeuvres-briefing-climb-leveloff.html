1
Climb Speeds
Best rate vs. cruise climb vs. rotation speeds
Theory Speed Reference Chart

Vy (Best Rate of Climb): 70 KIAS → ~822–855 ft/min ROC at MTOW, ISA. Maximum altitude gain per unit time.

Cruise Climb: 80 KIAS → Better forward visibility, engine cooling, slightly lower ROC than Vy but more practical for extended climbs.

VR (Rotate): 53 KIAS with Flap 1 — Rotation attitude only; do not hold this speed in flight.

Vy Safe (Positive ROC): 56 KIAS — Minimum speed after take-off showing positive rate of climb on VSI; safe to retract flaps only when passing this speed.

CAPA SOP Departure Sequence:

  • Rotate at 53 kt
  • Reach 56 kt → Flap retract → confirm positive ROC
  • Climb to 70 kt Vy
  • Transition to 80 kt cruise climb (if required)
Key Principle
After take-off: maintain MCP and 70 kt to 500 ft AGL minimum before any configuration change.
2
Climb Technique
Take-off and climb phases with configuration management
Manoeuvre Take-off & Climb Procedure

Take-Off Phase

  1. Full power — MCP (2250 RPM) — smooth throttle advance
  2. Rotate at 53 KIAS — raise nose to climb attitude
  3. Positive rate of climb confirmed (VSI, ALT)
  4. At 56 KIAS: Flap 1 → retract → confirm Vy 70 kt

Climb Phase

  1. Maintain 70 kt Vy — monitor AI pitch attitude reference
  2. Check ball centred — slight right rudder tendency on ROTAX climb
  3. At 500 ft AGL minimum: transition to 80 kt cruise climb if desired
  4. Lookout: 180° clearing turns every 500 ft in practice area
  5. Monitor engine temps: oil temp, coolant, EGT — all in green
3
Level-Off Technique
Altitude capture, sequence, and trim
Technique Altitude Capture & Transition to Cruise
Key Principle
Anticipate level-off = 10% of ROC before target altitude. At 800 ft/min ROC: start level-off 80 ft before target.

Sequence (Pitch → Wait → Power → Trim):

  1. Lower nose first to level attitude (stop the climb)
  2. Allow airspeed to build and altitude to stabilise
  3. THEN reduce power to cruise setting
  4. Apply trim for level cruise flight

Critical: Do NOT reduce power simultaneously with lowering nose — aircraft will decelerate and lose altitude.

Instructor Note
Never chase the altimeter on level-off — set the attitude first, the instruments will follow.
4
Power Schedule: Climb vs. Cruise
Configuration and power reduction sequence
SOP Power Settings & Configuration

Climb Phase

Power: MCP 2250 RPM

Configuration: Flap clean (after 56 kt)

Speed: 70–80 kt

Pitch: Climb attitude (AI reference)

Cruise Phase (Post Level-Off)

75% Power: 2060 RPM (~100–105 kt)

65% Power: 1975 RPM (~90–95 kt)

Configuration: Flap clean, trim set

Procedure: Allow speed to stabilise before reducing power further

SOP E2R1 §1.5 — Minimum altitude for power reduction after T/O: 500 ft AGL (clear of obstacles)

5
Debrief Criteria
Learning objectives assessment and feedback
Debrief Flight Performance Review
1 Was MCP maintained throughout the climb to 500 ft AGL?
2 Was Vy of 70 kt maintained ±5 kt from 56 kt onwards?
3 Was the level-off anticipation point correct (within ±100 ft of target)?
4 Was the pitch-first / power-second sequence used on level-off (no simultaneous reduction)?
5 Was trim applied correctly in each phase (climb, level-off, cruise)?
6 Was engine temperature monitoring conducted (oil, coolant, EGT all in green)?
7 Was cruise climb speed (80 kt) achieved smoothly if required?
8 Was ball centred throughout climb (right rudder applied as needed)?
⚠
Climb Lookout
In a climb, the nose obstructs forward view. Perform gentle clearing turns (10–15° each side) every 500 ft in uncontrolled airspace to check for traffic.
